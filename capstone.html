San Francisco United Proposal

Oregon State University Senior Capstone

This project was my Senior Project at Oregon State University, and I worked on it with a classmate (Brendan Spencer). The reason that we worked on this project together was to emulate the real world design environment, where designers very rarely work alone, but also because we knew our joint passions for design and soccer would help strengthen the final result.

The Proposal

Oregon State's Graphic Design Program has a Senior Capstone project that all designers complete prior to graduation. The professors structure it in an intentionally open ended manner, challenging us to find something we're passionate about and then creating something based on that. 

The topic that Brendan and I chose was the branding and all other collateral related to an expansion Major League Soccer (MLS) team. 

The Outcome

For this project, we started off by researching the state of sports in the United States through an infographic, after which we chose both a location and a team name for our team based off the results. Upon deciding on the name, we began creating the branding for this proposal, and then began implementing the branding to create a proposal. For our branding, it was centered around the phoenix, the symbol of San Francisco, and drew inspiration from the city as well as soccer in the United States and Internationally. For the proposal, we worked on creating jerseys, a website based on the MLS template, the front end design for the first of it's kind application for the MLS, as well as apparel and merchandise.

As part of our proposal, Brendan and I created a video for the city, using footage by San Francisco based videographers, showing our proposal and the story behind the team. (The video can be seen here)
