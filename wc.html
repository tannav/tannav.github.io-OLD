World Cup Final Data Visualization

Data Visualization 101 Class

This project was created as part of my curriculum at Oregon State University. As part of my Data Visualization class, we were tasked with finding a data source that interested us and then creating an infographic about it in the style of Edward Tufte.

The Proposal

As I have been an avid sports fan for years, I ended up selecting the 2014 World Cup Final between Argentina and Germany. My aim with this visualization was to show how close these two teams were, and how they both had opportunities to win the game, but that Germany squeaked away with the title.

The Outcome

Along the top of my infographic, I used data from FIFA to show key game statistics, including possession, corner kicks and fouls. In the middle third of my infographic, I created a timeline, allowing the 120 minute game to be seen at a glance, whether that be injuries, yellow cards, free kicks, or the lone goal. Along the bottom, I illustrated key players from both teams and created player performance pie charts, allowing for quick comparison of players in different aspects of the game.
